## Overview

Skin cancer is one of the most common and treatable cancers when detected early. This project develops a **deep learning pipeline** for **multi-class skin cancer classification** from dermoscopic images, with a focus on training stability, data augmentation, and model interpretability.

---

## Methodology

- **Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) Augmentation** — novel augmentation strategy using SGD-driven perturbations to improve training robustness on limited medical datasets
- **Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)** — deep architecture for multi-class dermoscopic image classification across skin lesion types
- **Activation Mapping (Grad-CAM)** — visual explanations highlighting image regions that drive the classification decision, critical for clinical trust

---

## Key Contributions

- SGD-based augmentation strategy tailored for limited medical imaging datasets
- Multi-class classification across multiple skin cancer types
- Activation mapping provides visually interpretable evidence for clinical review
- Competitive accuracy on benchmark dermoscopic datasets
